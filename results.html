Results

Small model training

Using the implementation detailed in the previous notebook, we trained a "small model" on a small subset of the training data. This amounted to 1,500 randomly sampled images from each class, 3,000 training images total.


Small model performance

We tested this small model on a very large test dataset of 30,000 test images (15,000 from each class). We found that this "small model" generalizes exceptionally well to unseen images, and classifies whether a stop sign has been vandalized or not with very high accuracy.

The CNN model demonstrates a high overall accuracy of 98.5% with an impressive true positive rate of over 99%. It is highly effective at correctly identifying non-graffitied stop signs and rarely misclassifies them as graffitied. These results highlight the small model's ability to generalize effectively to unseen images.
